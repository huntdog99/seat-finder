# Seat Finder — TikTok & Instagram Promotion Strategy

## The Core Angle

Every other flight search app competes on price. We compete on **comfort**. That's the hook. Nobody else lets you pick your exact seat type before you even book. We're not selling flights — we're selling the feeling of never getting stuck in a middle seat again.

---

## 1. Platform Strategy

### TikTok

**Format focus:** Short-form video (15-60 seconds), trending audio, POV/skit format
**Posting cadence:** 5-7x per week (algorithm rewards volume)
**Target audience:**
- Frequent flyers (25-45) who are tired of seat roulette
- Travel creators and "travel hack" communities
- Anxious flyers who need specific seats (window, front of plane)
- Business travelers who value aisle seats and legroom

**Content pillars:**
1. "Seat horror stories" — relatable pain-point content
2. "Did you know?" — educational hooks about seat selection
3. Product demos — fast, punchy screen recordings
4. Creator partnerships — travel influencers using the app

### Instagram

**Format focus:** Reels (primary), carousel posts (educational), Stories (engagement/polls)
**Posting cadence:** Reels 4-5x/week, Carousels 2x/week, Stories daily
**Target audience:** Same core demo, skewing slightly older (28-50), higher intent, aspirational travel crowd

**Content pillars:**
1. Reels — same short-form content repurposed from TikTok with IG-native edits
2. Carousels — "5 seats you should never book" educational content
3. Stories — polls ("Window or aisle?"), quizzes, behind-the-scenes
4. UGC — repost user screenshots of finding their perfect seat

## 2. Creative Concepts

### Concept 1: "The Middle Seat Nightmare" (TikTok/Reels)

**Hook:** "POV: You just realized you're in a middle seat for 6 hours"
**Format:** POV skit, trending audio, 15 seconds
**Script:**
- Camera on person looking at boarding pass, zoom into middle seat assignment
- Cut to cramped middle seat between two large passengers
- Text overlay: "This could have been avoided"
- Cut to Seat Finder app — user selects "Window" + "Emergency Row"
- End card: "Stop gambling on seats. SeatFinder.com"

**Why it works:** Universal pain point. Highly shareable. The "before/after" format drives product understanding instantly.

### Concept 2: "Seat Secrets Airlines Don't Tell You" (TikTok/Reels)

**Hook:** "3 things about airplane seats the airlines don't want you to know"
**Format:** Talking head + text overlay, educational, 30-45 seconds
**Script:**
- "Number 1: Bulkhead seats have 40% more legroom but you can't store bags under the seat"
- "Number 2: Emergency row seats are wider on most aircraft"
- "Number 3: Front-half seats deplane 15 minutes faster on average"
- "The only app that lets you search by ALL of these? Seat Finder."

**Why it works:** "Secret knowledge" format is algorithmic gold on TikTok. Positions us as the authority on seat selection.

### Concept 3: "Window vs. Aisle" Debate Series (TikTok/IG)

**Hook:** "The window vs. aisle debate ends NOW"
**Format:** Split-screen debate, comment-bait, series content
**Script:**
- Side-by-side of window person enjoying view vs. aisle person stretching legs
- Each "side" makes their case with text overlays
- End: "Whatever side you're on — Seat Finder gets you YOUR seat. Every time."
- CTA: Poll in comments ("Drop a [plane emoji] for window, [leg emoji] for aisle")

**Why it works:** Debate content drives massive engagement. Comment sections explode. Series format = returning viewers. Naturally introduces the product as the solution for both sides.

### Concept 4: "I Tested Every Seat on the Plane" (TikTok/YouTube Short)

**Hook:** "I flew 12 flights in 30 days to find the best seat on the plane"
**Format:** Documentary-style montage, 45-60 seconds
**Script:**
- Quick cuts of different seats: bulkhead, emergency, window front, aisle back
- Rate each with on-screen scorecard
- Reveal "winner" seat combo
- "Want to actually book these seats? There's only one app that searches by seat type."

**Why it works:** High production value stands out. "I tested it so you don't have to" format builds trust. Perfect for creator partnerships.

### Concept 5: "The $200 Seat Hack" (TikTok/Reels)

**Hook:** "This free app saved me $200 on my last flight and got me a better seat"
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover, 30 seconds
**Script:**
- Open Seat Finder, type in JFK to LAX
- Select Window + Front Half
- Show results: cheaper flight WITH preferred seat vs. competitor showing expensive upgrade
- "Airlines charge $50-200 for seat selection. This app finds flights where your seat is already included."

**Why it works:** Money-saving angle is the #1 engagement driver in travel content. Demonstrates the product live. Concrete dollar amount in the hook stops the scroll.

## 3. Landing Page Recommendations

### Social Traffic Landing Page (separate from main app)

**URL:** seatfinder.com/go (short, memorable for social bios)

**Above the fold:**
- Headline: "Stop Gambling on Airplane Seats"
- Subhead: "The only flight search that lets you pick window, aisle, bulkhead, or emergency row — before you book."
- Single CTA button: "Find Your Seat" (scrolls to embedded search or opens app)
- Social proof: "Join X travelers who found their perfect seat"

**Below the fold:**
- 3-column feature grid: seat types with icons
- "How it works" in 3 steps (Search → Pick seat type → Book)
- Testimonial/review section
- FAQ (2-3 questions max)

**Design principles for Tony:**
- Mobile-first (90%+ of social traffic is mobile)
- Load time under 2 seconds (social users bounce fast)
- Dark mode / aviation-inspired aesthetic to match app
- Large tap targets, minimal form fields visible on landing
- Video hero option: embed the best-performing TikTok as social proof
- No navigation bar on landing page — single path to conversion

### Platform-specific landing variants:
- **TikTok link-in-bio:** seatfinder.com/tiktok — track attribution
- **Instagram link-in-bio:** seatfinder.com/instagram — track attribution
- Both should have UTM parameters baked in for analytics

## 4. Launch Sequence

**Week 1-2:** Seed content — post 3-5 "seat horror story" and educational pieces to build audience before product push
**Week 3:** Product reveal — drop Concept 1 and Concept 5 (highest viral potential)
**Week 4+:** Sustained cadence — mix of all 5 concepts, iterate based on what hits
**Ongoing:** Creator outreach — identify 5-10 travel micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) for partnerships
